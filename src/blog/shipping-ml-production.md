Shipping an ML model is not the hard part. Keeping it working six months later — across data that looks nothing like your training set, without a team of five to babysit it — that is the hard part.

These are the failure modes I've seen consistently across production ML deployments, not in the models themselves but in the infrastructure and expectations around them.

## Where Projects Actually Break

- **Feature drift** — training features that silently change schema or semantics in production
- **Orchestration complexity** — pipelines that work once but not reliably on a schedule
- **Weak monitoring** — knowing the model ran but not whether it ran correctly
- **Mismatched expectations** — stakeholders treating ML output as ground truth rather than a signal
